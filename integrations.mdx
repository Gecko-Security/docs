## Connect integrations in the right order

Do not connect everything on day one. Add integrations in the order that makes
the scan workflow stronger:

1. Source control
2. Team workflow tools
3. Notifications
4. Access and provisioning

Gecko works best when integrations follow the scan workflow instead of
preceding it.

## Source control

### GitHub and GitLab

Use **Settings** &gt; **Git Settings** to connect your source-control provider.

After the provider is connected:

- Gecko can load connected repositories directly in the scan flow.
- Teams can choose repositories and branches without pasting raw URLs.
- Repository-backed scans become easier to repeat and schedule.

<Tip>
  Start by connecting the provider your team already uses for private code.
  Use public OSS URLs only when you are evaluating Gecko on a public project.
</Tip>

## Team workflow integrations

### Jira

Use **Settings** &gt; **Jira** when your team tracks remediation work in Jira.

Connect Jira after your team already knows how it wants to triage findings.
That keeps the integration useful instead of noisy.

### Linear

Use **Settings** &gt; **Linear** if your engineering organization works in
Linear.

Connect it when you want Gecko findings to move cleanly into your normal
engineering backlog and ownership process.

### ClickUp

Use **Settings** &gt; **ClickUp** if ClickUp is where your team plans and tracks
fix work.

Use it the same way you would use Jira or Linear: to make remediation visible
and owned.

### Shortcut

Use **Settings** &gt; **Shortcut** if your team manages engineering work there.

Connect it after you have a stable triage process so only real issues become
tracked work.

## Notification integrations

### Slack

Use **Settings** &gt; **Slack** or **Settings** &gt; **Integrations** to connect
Slack.

Slack is best for timely scan notifications, follow-up nudges, and making sure
the right team sees new security work without living inside Gecko all day.

## Access and provisioning

### Okta SAML and SCIM

Use **Settings** &gt; **Single Sign-On &amp; SCIM** for team access control.

Set up SSO after your team is actively using Gecko, not before your first scan.

When you are ready, follow the dedicated [Okta SAML onboarding](/access/okta-saml-onboarding)
guide.

## Other integration surfaces

### GitLab vulnerability export

If your team needs findings to show up in GitLab-native security workflows, use
**Settings** &gt; **GitLab vulnerabilities** after your main scan loop is
already stable.

### API reference

If you are automating around Gecko, use the [API Reference](/api-reference/introduction)
for endpoints, request schemas, and response fields.

## Recommended setup checklist

<Check>
  Connect one code host first, run a real scan, fix at least one finding, and
  only then add trackers, notifications, and SSO.
</Check>
