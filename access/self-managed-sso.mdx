Self-managed SSO lets a team admin set up single sign-on entirely from inside
Gecko, with no back-and-forth with the Gecko team. It works with any SAML 2.0 or OIDC
identity provider; you choose the protocol when you start, and configure the rest
in the setup portal.

<Info>
  **You'll need:** Gecko **Enterprise** plan, a Gecko team admin, an admin on your
  identity provider, and a test user who isn't the admin changing sign-in policy.
</Info>

## Set it up

<Steps>
<Step title="Save your team slug">
  In **Settings > Single Sign-On & SCIM**, save a unique team slug. It becomes
  your connection identifier and sign-in path, so your service-provider values are
  based on it.

  <Warning>
    The slug is **read-only once SSO is connected**. Pick the final one now.
  </Warning>
</Step>

<Step title="Start the connection">
  Click **Connect** and choose **SAML** or **OIDC**. Gecko opens the setup portal,
  which shows the values your IdP needs:
  - **SAML:** ACS URL and Entity ID
  - **OIDC:** the redirect (callback) URI
</Step>

<Step title="Create the app in your IdP">
  Create a SAML or OIDC app in your identity provider using those values.
  - **SAML:** set the Name ID and attributes. See
    [SAML SSO Setup](/access/saml) for the exact field values.
  - **OIDC:** create an OAuth client and note its **client ID**, **client
    secret**, and **issuer / discovery URL**.

  Assign the app to the users who should reach Gecko.
</Step>

<Step title="Finish in the portal">
  Enter your IdP details back in the setup portal:
  - **SAML:** sign-in URL and signing certificate
  - **OIDC:** issuer/discovery URL, client ID, and client secret
</Step>

<Step title="Verify and test">
  Return to Gecko and **verify**. The connection moves to **Connected** and Gecko
  shows your team **Sign-in URL**. Open it in a fresh browser and log in with a
  **non-admin** user.

  Keep one admin session open and **SSO enforcement** optional until this works,
  then switch enforcement to **Required**.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Want Gecko to set the connection up for you instead? See
  [SAML SSO Setup](/access/saml) for the managed exchange, where you send us your
  IdP details and we activate it.
</Note>
